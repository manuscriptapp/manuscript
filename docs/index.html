An open-source writing app for people who care about their files

Manuscript is a native iOS and macOS app for long-form writing. It's free, the source code is available under MPL-2.0, and your work stays in standard Markdown files you control.

View on GitHub
Why Manuscript

Built for writers who value ownership

Scrivener and Ulysses are good tools, but they're closed-source and cost money. Manuscript gives you similar capabilities without the license fees or vendor lock-in.

Open source

The entire codebase is public under MPL-2.0. Read it, modify it, contribute to it. No black boxes.

Free forever

No purchase price, no subscription, no in-app purchases. The app is free and will stay that way.

Your files, your way

Projects are folders of Markdown files. Open them in any text editor, version them with Git, back them up however you want.

iCloud sync included

CloudKit keeps your projects in sync across Mac, iPhone, and iPad. No account to create, no extra cost.

Capabilities

What you can do with it

Manuscript handles the core workflows that matter for long-form writing: organizing, drafting, revising, and exporting.

Project binder Organize chapters, scenes, notes, and research in a sidebar
Markdown editing Write in plain Markdown with live preview
Snapshots Automatic version history so you can always go back
Split editor View two documents side by side
Composition mode Distraction-free fullscreen writing
Export PDF, DOCX, EPUB, HTML, LaTeX
Writing targets Set word count goals for sessions and projects
Tags and labels Organize and filter documents your way

AI assistance

Optional, private, flexible

AI features are there if you want them. They're not required, and you control how they work.

Apple Foundation Models

On-device AI through Apple Intelligence. Your text never leaves your Mac or iPhone. No API costs.

Bring your own keys

Connect to OpenAI, Anthropic, or other providers using your own API keys. Configure per-project.

File format

Plain folders, plain files

A Manuscript project is just a folder. Inside are Markdown files for your writing and JSON files for metadata. Nothing proprietary.

You can open any document in another editor, commit the whole project to Git, or write scripts to process your files. The format is documented and won't change in breaking ways.

MyNovel.manuscript/ ├── project.json ├── contents/ │ ├── draft/ │ │ ├── 01-chapter-one.md │ │ └── 02-chapter-two.md │ ├── notes/ │ └── research/ ├── snapshots/ └── assets/
